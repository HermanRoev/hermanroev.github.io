What is Virtual Reality

Virtual Reality is a technology which uses either multi projected enviroments or virtual reality headsets to immerse the user into a virtual enviroment. The VR headset uses a couple of near eye displays and position tracking, combined with spatial audio to give the user enough sensational feedback to dive into the virtual world. The position tracking is usually done with either base stations or on headset sensors. Often including a couple of handheld controllers to track hand movement. Though full body tracking solutions also exsist.

Application of Virtual Reality

Virtual reality technology has many use cases in which they offer great benefits. In education virtual reality technology can be used to give training in fields where operating in the real world demands a lot of resources or includes dangerous situations, like the military or in medical practice. In engineering virtual reality or augmented reality is used to get a better understanding of what's being built. For example, doing a virtual walkthrough of a ship or a building, finding better solutions for layouts. The most mainstream usage of virtual reality comes in the form of recreational use, like in videogames, or watching content with 360 views.

What is a Metaverse

The term Metaverse originates from the 1992 novel Snow Crash, a combination of the words meta and universe. In pop culture the metaverse is a term used to descbribe the virtual world, enhanced with Virtual or Augmented reality. The hit movie Ready Player One is an example of a world where the metaverse is a sentral part of society.

The Meta Metaverse

Meta is currently developing their Meta "metaverse", a place that they intend to be a virtual enviroment that will interact with most of our day to day activities. The metaverse will give people digital spaces made to help conect people accross the real and virtual world. Meta is planing on using both Augmented Reality and Virtual reality to make work, games, learning and socalising easier.
